## Section 7 — Transport and Tempo

The transport is global, carrying master BPM, a beat counter, and play/stop/rewind state. There is one master BPM per score, held in scene.json's bpm field at the score level. Every source's wall-clock cycle period is derived at runtime as beatsPerCycle ÷ master BPM × 60 seconds; a tempo change rescales every source's cycle pacing automatically without per-source adjustment.

Per-source cycle. Each source has its own beatsPerCycle (default 4) giving the cycle length in master beats. Sources can therefore run at different cycle periods independently while sharing the one master tempo, which is the basis for polyrhythmic and polymetric textures across sources on the same canvas. Triggers carry the field too but their cycle counter is internal-only since triggers cannot self-fire under the cursor-as-collider model.

Duration specification. The inspector's cycle duration field surfaces beatsPerCycle as "Cycles In [N] beats" in Band 1. A planned extension will allow durations to be specified not just as integer beats but as musical subdivisions of beats (for example "16 eighth notes" or "9 triplet quarter notes"); this is not yet implemented. Within-cycle event density is expressed by Strudel mini-notation modifiers in the cyclePattern itself (see Section 10 for the curated modifier vocabulary and Section 12 for the evaluation flow), so the duration field handles cycle scope only.

Determinism. The transport's job is to advance a beat counter; the simulation's evolution is exclusively a function of (initial scene state, elapsed beats), with no dependence on wall-clock timing during the run. Rewind resets every piece of dynamic state (sprite positions, cursor positions, behaviour-internal counters) to whatever the scene declares as initial state, so a piece played from beat zero through to beat N produces the identical sequence of events on every replay regardless of how fast the audio callback runs in wall time. This determinism is the foundation for repeatable composition and for offline rendering at faster than realtime.

Transport bar. The transport controls live in a bar along the bottom of the main window: a rewind button and a play-pause toggle on the left, followed by an elapsed-time readout and a BPM field. BPM is editable at runtime from the bar for live experimentation. The transport clock is driven by the browser's AudioContext.currentTime, which provides sub-millisecond timing accuracy independent of animation frame timing.
